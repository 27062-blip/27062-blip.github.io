What this law stands for

Four key rights

01

Know

People must know when AI is shaping a life-changing decision.

02

Challenge

People must be able to ask a real person to review the result.

03

Safety

High-risk AI must be tested before it can harm people at scale.

04

Oversight

Powerful systems must stay open to public review and scrutiny.

The problem

The problem

AI already decides who gets seen, trusted, hired, approved, or ignored. When those decisions are hidden, people can be harmed without ever getting an explanation.

Hidden decisions

People can be judged by systems they never even knew were there.

Fast harm

One broken system can spread unfairness across thousands of lives.

No appeal

When the machine gets it wrong, many people have nowhere to turn.

Why it matters

This is about people, not software.

A rejected worker. A denied student. A family refused help. Behind every automated decision is a human life that deserves fairness.

Voting for this law means saying that speed is not more important than dignity, and convenience is not more important than justice.

Main rules

Main rules

Rule 1

Groups must say when AI is used in major decisions.

Rule 2

People must be able to request human review.

Rule 3

High-risk AI must pass safety and fairness checks.

Rule 4

Public agencies must keep records for audits.
