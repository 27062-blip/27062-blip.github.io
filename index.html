Policy summary

Legislative overview

Title of the law

Citizens' AI Rights Act.

What AI problem it solves

Addresses opaque AI decisions that may unfairly affect employment, education, credit, and essential services.

Main rules of the law

Requires AI-use disclosure, guarantees human review rights, and mandates safety checks for high-risk systems.

Who benefits

Workers, students, families, consumers, and responsible technology providers.

Possible criticisms

Potential concerns include compliance burden, broad scope, and implementation capacity.

Why voters should support it

It strengthens transparency, accountability, and procedural rights where AI affects real outcomes.

What this law does

Core legal safeguards

01

Know

Individuals must be informed when AI is used in high-impact decisions.

02

Challenge

Individuals may request formal human review.

03

Safety

High-risk AI systems must pass pre-deployment checks.

Why this matters

Human rights in automated systems

No one should lose access to opportunity, services, or fair treatment due to an unaccountable algorithm.

This framework aligns innovation with due process, transparency, and public oversight.
