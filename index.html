What this law stands for

Four key rights

01

Know

People must be told when AI affects a major decision.

02

Challenge

People must be able to ask for human review.

03

Safety

High-risk AI must pass checks before wide use.

04

Oversight

Changes, comments, and support should stay public.

The problem

The problem

AI already affects jobs, school, credit, health, and public services. Too often, people do not know how decisions were made or how to challenge them.

Hidden decisions

AI can reject or score people without notice.

Fast harm

Bad systems can spread unfair results very quickly.

No appeal

People often have no clear way to challenge mistakes.

Main rules

Main rules

Rule 1

Groups must say when AI is used in major decisions.

Rule 2

People must be able to request human review.

Rule 3

High-risk AI must pass safety and fairness checks.

Rule 4

Public agencies must keep records for audits.

Who benefits

Who benefits

Workers

More clarity in hiring and workplace scoring.

Students

Better protection in admissions and school systems.

Consumers

Clearer answers on loans, insurance, and services.

Good builders

Clear rules reward safer companies and products.

Possible criticisms

Criticisms

Too broad
